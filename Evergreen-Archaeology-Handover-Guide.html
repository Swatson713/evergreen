Your Website Guide

A plain-English walkthrough for getting your site live, connecting your domain, and keeping your content up to date — no technical experience needed.

Prepared by your web developer  ·  May 2026

Before you begin

A quick note on how this works

Your website is made up of files — text, images, settings — that are stored in an online filing cabinet called GitHub. When those files change, GitHub automatically rebuilds and republishes your site. You never need to touch the files directly.

To update your site content (text, photos, projects), you'll use a tool called Keystatic — a simple admin panel that looks like a form. You fill in the fields, click Save, and your site updates within about a minute.

👋 How to read this guide

  • Steps marked Your developer are things your developer handles — you just need to know they're done.
  • Steps marked You are things you do yourself, in a web browser, with no technical knowledge required.

Step 1

Create your GitHub account

✓ You do this

GitHub is where your website files are stored. Think of it like Dropbox, but specifically designed for websites. You need a free account in your name so you own your site.

1
Go to github.com

Open your web browser and go to github.com. Click the green Sign up button.

2
Create a free account

Enter your email address, create a password, and choose a username. Use something professional like evergreenarch or your own name. The free plan is all you need.

3
Verify your email

GitHub will send you a confirmation email. Click the link inside it to activate your account.

Keep your GitHub username and password somewhere safe — you'll need them to access your site's admin panel.

4
Share your username with your developer

Send your GitHub username to your developer. They'll upload your website files into your account and set everything else up.

Step 2

Your developer uploads the site

Your developer does this

Once you've shared your GitHub username, your developer will upload all the website files into your account and set up the automatic publishing system. This takes about 10–15 minutes on their end.

When it's done, you'll receive a temporary web address that looks something like:
https://yourusername.github.io/evergreenarch

You can open that link in your browser to see the live site before your real domain is connected.

Step 3

Turn on GitHub Pages

✓ You do this

GitHub Pages is the free hosting service that makes your site available on the internet. You need to switch it on once — after that it runs itself automatically.

1
Open your repository on GitHub

Log into github.com. You'll see your repository (the filing cabinet of your site files) listed on the left side of your dashboard. Click on it.

2
Go to Settings

Along the top of the repository page, you'll see a row of tabs. Click Settings (it's near the right end, with a gear icon).

3
Find the Pages section

In the left-hand menu that appears, scroll down until you see Pages. Click on it.

4
Change the Source to "GitHub Actions"

Under Build and deployment, you'll see a dropdown currently set to "Deploy from a branch". Click it and select GitHub Actions.

This tells GitHub to use the automatic publishing system your developer already set up inside the project.

5
Your site is live!

Within about 60 seconds, your site will be published. Scroll back to the top of the Pages settings — you'll see a green banner with your site address. Click it to visit your live site.

⏱ From now on, the site updates itself

Every time your developer (or Keystatic) makes a change, the site automatically rebuilds and goes live within about 60 seconds. You don't need to come back to this Settings page again.

Step 4

Connect your domain name

Right now your site is at a GitHub address. This step swaps that for your real domain (e.g. evergreenarchaeology.com). It involves two things: telling GitHub about your domain, and telling your domain about GitHub.

Part A — Tell GitHub your domain name

✓ You do this
1
Go back to Settings → Pages

In your repository on GitHub, click Settings → Pages again.

2
Enter your domain in the "Custom domain" box

Type your full domain name (e.g. evergreenarchaeology.com) and click Save.

3
Tick "Enforce HTTPS" once it appears

After a few minutes a checkbox labelled Enforce HTTPS will appear. Tick it — this gives your site a padlock and keeps visitors' connections secure.

Part B — Point your domain at GitHub

✓ You do this (at your domain registrar)

Log in to the website where you bought your domain — this could be GoDaddy, Namecheap, Porkbun, Google Domains, or similar. Look for DNS Settings or Manage DNS. Add the following records exactly as shown:

Record TypeName / HostValue / Points To
A@185.199.108.153
A@185.199.109.153
A@185.199.110.153
A@185.199.111.153
CNAMEwwwyourusername.github.io

Replace yourusername with your actual GitHub username in the CNAME row.

🕐 DNS takes time to spread worldwide

After saving these records, it can take anywhere from a few minutes to 48 hours before your domain points to your site everywhere in the world. This is completely normal — it's like waiting for a postal address change to update across all the postal databases. Just check back the next day if it's not working immediately.

💡 Not sure where your DNS settings are?

Search Google for: "how to add DNS records in [your registrar name]" — every registrar has slightly different menus but the same records apply. Alternatively, send the table above to your developer and they can walk you through it.

Tell your developer the domain is set

Your developer does this last bit

Once your domain is connected, let your developer know. They need to update one line of code so that the site's internal links, sitemap, and search engine settings all use your real domain. This takes them about two minutes.

Step 5 — Ongoing

Managing your content with Keystatic

⚠️ Important — how content updates work with GitHub Pages

GitHub Pages is a "static" host, which means the Keystatic admin panel only works when your developer is running the site on their computer. To update content, you contact your developer with the changes, they make them in Keystatic and publish — the site updates automatically within a minute.

If you'd like to make content changes yourself without involving a developer, ask about upgrading to Cloudflare Pages hosting — it's also free and unlocks the full self-service Keystatic editor at your own domain.

What you can change through Keystatic

🏠 Hero Section

Headline, tagline, background image, and button labels on the homepage

👤 About Section

Your firm description, headshot or field photo, and credential badges

📬 Contact Details

Email address, phone number, office location, and contact form

📄 CV / Resume

Upload a PDF — the download button appears on the site automatically

🗂 Field Projects

Add, edit, or remove portfolio projects with photos and descriptions

🖼 Social Share Image

The image that appears when someone shares your site on LinkedIn or Facebook

Adding a new field project — step by step

This is the task you'll do most often. Here's exactly how it works:

1
Contact your developer

Let them know you want to add a new project. Send them the details — project name, location, date, type, a written summary, and any photos you want to include.

2
Developer opens Keystatic and fills in the form

They go to the Field Projects section in Keystatic, click Create entry, and fill in all the details you provided. Photos get uploaded directly through the form.

3
Developer clicks Save and publishes

One click to save, one command to publish. The site rebuilds automatically.

4
Your new project appears on the site within 60 seconds

You can visit your site and see the new project card appear in the portfolio grid. If you marked it as "Featured", it will appear as the large highlighted card at the top of the projects page.

Setting up the contact form

The contact form on your site uses a free service called Formspree, which forwards messages directly to your email. To activate it:

1
Go to formspree.io and create a free account

Use your business email address. The free plan allows 50 messages per month — more than enough to start.

2
Create a new form

Click New Form, give it a name like "Website Contact", and click Create. Formspree will give you a short Form ID (e.g. xpwzgkqr).

3
Send the Form ID to your developer

They'll paste it into the Contact Section settings in Keystatic. The contact form will then appear on your live site and all submissions will be forwarded to your email.

Ongoing

Day-to-day — what to expect

✅ Your site runs itself

Once it's live, your website needs no maintenance from you. It doesn't need updates, security patches, or restarts. It just works.

When you want to make a change

Simply get in touch with your developer and describe what you need. Common requests include:

  • Add a new project to the portfolio
  • Update your contact email or phone number
  • Replace your about photo or CV
  • Change the homepage headline or tagline
  • Mark a project as "Featured" to highlight it

What happens after a change is made

Your developer makes the change in Keystatic, publishes it, and your site is live within 60 seconds. You don't need to do anything — just check the live site to confirm it looks right.

If something looks broken

Take a screenshot of what you're seeing and send it to your developer with a description of the problem. In almost all cases it can be fixed with a small change and published within minutes.
